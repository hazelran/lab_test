Research

VCP-Lab investigates the full pipeline of video and image coding — from standardization-level algorithm design to AI-based enhancement — with a strong focus on real-world deployment and international standards impact.

Video Coding Standards

We actively contribute to international video coding standards through JVET (Joint Video Experts Team) and MPEG. Our work spans VVC (H.266), HEVC (H.265), AVC (H.264), AV1, and EVC — developing faster algorithms, lower-complexity implementations, and novel coding tools.

  • Fast partitioning and low-complexity intra/inter coding
  • Input contribution to JVET and MPEG standard meetings
  • Essential Video Coding (EVC) standard contributions

AI-based Video Enhancement

We develop deep learning models — including CNN, GAN, and Transformer architectures — to enhance the quality of compressed video and images as in-loop or post-processing filters.

  • Multi-scale self-attention post-filters for AV1 and VVC
  • GAN-based artifact reduction for compressed video
  • Dual-path residual gating for high-resolution video

Video Coding for Machines (VCM)

As machine vision becomes pervasive, we study how to compress visual data optimized for downstream AI tasks rather than human perception. This includes the MPEG FCVCM (Feature Coding for Video and Image Coding for Machines) standard.

  • Feature compression for machine inference
  • VVC coding tools for Video Coding for Machines
  • MPEG FCVCM standard contributions

Immersive & Next-Generation Media

We look ahead to 4K/8K/16K ultra-high-resolution video, virtual reality (VR), augmented reality (AR), and 360° omnidirectional video — developing compression and processing technologies to support the next generation of immersive media experiences.

  • Super-resolution based video coding
  • Metaverse content compression (IITP project)
  • Depth coding for 3D and immersive video
